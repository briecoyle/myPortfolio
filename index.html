About Me


I am a fullstack web developer, Flatiron School alumna, addicted hobbyist, and proponent of the Oxford comma.

I spent the past two years as a high school biology teacher. I was the first instructor at my institution to fully flip my classroom, recording my lectures as videos, assigning them as homework and spending class time to work on problem sets and labs. It was actually my experience in the classroom that led me to my true passion of web development.

One summer, I made the decision to gamify my classroom. I wanted to offer incentives to my students for homework streaks and perfect quiz scores through the use of badges and experience points. Because I could not find a tool that suited all of my needs, I decided that I would just make one myself. While I might have bitten off more than I could chew, I fell in love with the process of programming. I found that I quickly became fascinated by making applications from the first 'hello, world' rendered in my browser. In November 2016, I decided to professionally pursue web development as a career and enrolled in an online, self-paced program for fullstack Ruby on Rails and JavaScript. I was able to balance working full-time as a teacher with my studies, and I filled all of my extra time with coding. I am proud to have over 500 repositories on GitHub and a full-stack developer byline on LinkedIn now.
